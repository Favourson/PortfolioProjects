Discovering the arts and humanities

Description of Course 1: Discovering the arts and humanities: This course provides an introduction to the arts and humanities, exploring their significance and impact on society. Students will engage with a range of disciplines, including literature, history, philosophy, and art.

Prerequisites: Course 2, Course 3

Early modern Europe: society and culture c.1500-1780

Description of Course 2: Early modern Europe: society and culture c.1500-1780: This course explores the history of Europe from the Renaissance to the Enlightenment. Students will learn about the social and cultural changes that occurred during this period, including the rise of nationalism, the emergence of modern science, and the growth of the printing industry.

Prerequisites: None

Art and visual cultures in the modern world

Description of Course 3: Art and visual cultures in the modern world: This course examines the evolution of art and visual cultures from the late 19th century to the present day. Students will explore a range of artistic movements and styles, from modernism to postmodernism, and will consider the social and political contexts in which they emerged.

Prerequisites: Course 2

Exploring art and visual culture

Description of Course 4: Exploring art and visual culture: This course provides an introduction to the study of art and visual culture, covering topics such as art history, aesthetics, and the interpretation of visual texts. Students will develop critical thinking skills and learn to analyze and evaluate different forms of visual communication.

Prerequisites: Course 3

Engineering: mathematics, modelling, applications

Description of Course 5: Engineering: mathematics, modelling, applications: This course covers the fundamental principles of engineering, with a focus on mathematics, modeling, and real-world applications. Mechanical engineering: heat and flow

Description of Course 6: Mechanical engineering: heat and flow: This course focuses on the principles of thermodynamics and fluid mechanics, with a focus on practical applications in mechanical engineering. Essential economics: macro and micro perspectives

Description of Course 7: Essential economics: macro and micro perspectives: This course provides an introduction to economics, covering both macroeconomic and microeconomic principles.
